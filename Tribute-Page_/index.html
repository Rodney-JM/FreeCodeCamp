Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla, the misunderstood visionary

Nikola Tesla, the misunderstood visionary, illuminated the world with his brilliant mind, laying the groundwork for the modern era of electricity and technology, while his bold ideas transcend time, echoing as sparks of genius in the history of humanity.

  • July 10, 1856: Nikola Tesla was born in Smiljan, part of the Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 875-1878: Tesla studied electrical engineering at the Technical University in Graz, Austria, but left before completing his degree.
  • 1881: Emigrated to the United States and started working with Thomas Edison at the Edison Machine Works.
  • 1884: Moved to New York and began working independently. Patented several inventions during this period, including an electromagnetic induction motor.
  • 1888: Developed the concept of alternating current (AC) and polyphase system, enabling efficient long-distance transmission of electricity. Sold his AC patent to George Westinghouse.
  • 1891: Became a naturalized citizen of the United States.
  • 1893: The Chicago World's Columbian Exposition featured Tesla's alternating current system, showcasing the benefits of large-scale electrical power transmission.
  • 1895: Tesla started working at his laboratory in New York, known as Wardenclyffe Tower, where he planned to develop wireless energy transmission.
  • 1899: Conducted experiments at Wardenclyffe Tower to transmit wireless energy, but funding ran out, and the project was abandoned.
  • 1901: Marconi received credit for the invention of the radio, leading to a dispute with Tesla, who claimed his patents covered many aspects of the technology.
  • 1915: Tesla declared personal bankruptcy and had to sell many of his possessions.
  • January 7, 1943: Nikola Tesla passed away in New York at the age of 86.
